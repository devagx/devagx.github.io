Return To Tech

I have always had a keen interest in computing from an early age and pursued my interest academically through school and college right through to university where I studied for a BSc (Hons) Computing degree where I graduated with a first-class honour’s degree. Shortly after graduating I managed to successfully secure a software development role at a company called Digital Vision where I had the opportunity to develop custom web-based and desktop applications using C#.NET, VB.NET, VB6, SQL Server and Oracle as well as develop middleware solutions connecting third party financial systems such as SAP to an invoice Automation product called Contempus. Another aspect to my role was consultancy where I would provide professional services to customers who had purchased the Contempus product in the form of product configurations and advise best practice business processes.

After two years working for the company, the company was acquired by a company called Basware and I automatically transitioned into the new company in a similar role matching the consulting and middleware development aspects of my previous role, however the development of custom web based and desktop applications did not form part of the new role. In addition to this the development of the middleware solutions were no longer being developed in .NET or VB6 but instead developed using an in-house programming language.

Having worked at Basware for over 12 years I came to realise my software development skills were not really transferable and soon became unfamiliar with all the new versions of .NET and was no longer passionate about developing middleware solutions in a technology only known to the company I was working for. In February 2020 I made a decision I wanted to return to a more software development focused role with minimal to no consultancy-based duties, and to start once again developing software using the latest technologies such as C# .NET or Java and regain my passion I once had developing software.

Having spent 4 to 6 weeks looking for a software development role I soon realised my technical knowledge was not where it needed to be in order to be successful in a senior software development role, having not being able to complete technical interview tests to the required standards for the positions I was applying for. I found I was able to think about the required solutions for the task, but lacked in knowledge of features from new versions of .NET and general syntax. At this point in time, I found myself in a challenging position in that I was struggling to perform well in technical interviews for senior positions and shortly after it became apparent most businesses were no longer hiring new employees due to the COVID 19 pandemic and country lock down.

At this point I was not aware of the Return To Tech programme and made a decision to work on cyber security certifications so I could deepen my awareness of the topic in general with it being a particular passion of mine, but in particular in the areas of developing secure software applications and API's. I worked on and achieved certifications for almost one year and by luck towards the end of 2020 I found out about the Return to Tech programme and thought this would be an ideal opportunity to get back into senior software development roles and strengthen my coding skills, and in addition be a good opportunity to learn some new technolgies concentrating on the front-end software development using tech such as React and have an opportunity to participate in activities such as paired programming which I have not done before.
